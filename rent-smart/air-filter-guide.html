How Often Should You Replace an Apartment Air Filter?

5 min read • Updated 2026

Short answer: Every 30–90 days.

Here’s the deal — if you ignore your air filter, your HVAC system works harder, your air quality drops, and you’re basically paying extra to breathe dust.

Why It Actually Matters

A clogged filter restricts airflow. That means:

  • Higher electric bills
  • More dust inside your apartment
  • Extra strain on the HVAC system
  • Potential maintenance complaints

Some leases require you to replace filters regularly. Ignore it long enough and you could be blamed for system damage.

Big Brother Rule

Keep it simple:

• No pets → Every 60 days
• Pets → Every 30 days
• Dusty area → Every 30 days
• Allergy issues → Every 30–45 days

Set a calendar reminder and stop thinking about it.

How to Replace It (Takes 3 Minutes)

  1. Turn off the thermostat.
  2. Remove the old filter.
  3. Check the size printed on the frame.
  4. Insert the new filter with the airflow arrow pointing inward.
  5. Turn the system back on.

Deposit Protection Tip

Take a photo of the new filter after replacing it. If maintenance ever questions it, you have proof.
